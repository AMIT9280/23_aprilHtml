Virat Kohli (Hindi pronunciation: [ʋɪˈɾɑːʈ ˈkoːɦli] (listen); born 5 November 1988) is an

Indian international cricketer and the former captain of the Indian national cricket team

who plays as a right-handed batsman for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL and for the Delhi in Indian domestic cricket. He is regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket.[4] He is the second batsman to score most number of international centuries in cricket. Holding the record for most runs in T20 internationals and in the IPL, the International Cricket Council named him the male cricketer of the decade. Kohli has also contributed to a number of India's successes, including winning the 2011 World Cup and the 2013 Champions trophy.
